Our Privacy Policy

ReadyEdgeGo (the "Company") wants you to be aware of how we collect, protect, and use your personally identifiable information ("your information").

Online Information

Our goal is to provide an enjoyable online experience. When you access a Company website ("Company Site"), the Company follows procedures to physically and electonically protect your information. We will identify the Internet Protocol (IP) address of your computer or handheld device in order to identify the device you're using. We store IP addresses for security purposes only.

If you provide us with your email address, we may notify you of other products and services. If you email us, we will use your email address to reply to you and we will temporarily store conversations to communicate effectively and to satisfy applicable laws and regulations.

Company Sites don't knowingly collect information from children under age 13 unless first complying with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act. Whenever we discover we have unknowingly collected such information we will immediately terminate all traces of this information.

We are not responisble for your privacy and your information beyond our Company Sites.

What Information We Collect And Why

In order to communicate with you about our products and services, we collect:

  • Information you provide to us by email, by telephone, in writing, or through our Company Sites.
  • Information collected from your transactions with us.

How We Protect Your Information

We are committed to securing your information.

  • Our employees use your information only to the extent required to perform their work.
  • We comply with and exceed federal and state regulations to protect your information.

Sharing Information with Partner and Non-Partner Organizations

We may share your information with our partners and third-parties if and only if you provide us with written or oral consent to do so. We may also share your information to comply with a legal requirement, such as a law, regulation, court order, subpoena, search warrant, or in the course of a legal proceeding.

How You Can Protect Your Privacy

If you feel you have been targeted for identity theft, we recommend the following options:

  • File a police report with your local authorities
  • Call the Federal Social Security Administration fraud hotline: 800.269.0271
  • Call the Federal Trade Commission's Identity Theft hotline: 877.IDTHEFT
  • Contact the major national credit reporting agencies: Experian, Equifax, Trans Union, or Innovis.
