8 Steps to Take for College Applications

Whether you're applying for a private school or a public university, completing applications is a complex task.
Before you start, take a deep breath and look at our must-knows about college applications.

  1. Know your deadlines. The most well written application is as good as no application at all if it is late.

  2. Proofread. You have one chance to strike a good impression. The least you could do is check for typos and punctuation.

  3. Apply, even if you're not sure. Not sure if you'll get in? This is your one chance to apply - what have you got to lose?

  4. An average SAT score isn't the end. Admissions officers consider many other factors, including volunteer service, leadership, sports, and course selection.

  5. Apply earlier. A tremendous amount of students submit their applications near the deadline, creating a massive headache for admissions departments every year. Submit earlier and you'll have a higher chance of being remembered.

  6. Keep Cs off the transcript. Getting a C in an AP class might be equivalent to a B, but too many Cs will surely decrease your chances of admission anywhere. Challenge yourself but don't overdo it.

  7. Get feedback for your essay. Although the college essay is very personal, students should obtain as much constructive feedback as possible, just as they should for any school essay. Moreover, writing about yourself tends to be unfamiliar territory for any high schooler, making it even more important to seek help.

  8. Write a story, not a laundry list. Admissions officers read thousands of essays every year, and the only way to capture their attention is to tell a memorable story. Listing out your accomplishments will only portray weak writing skills and an inability to follow directions, as your accomplishments should be listed in other parts of your application.
